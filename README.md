# PRCS Strategy Intelligence Lab

An illustrative interactive proof of concept developed for the proposed Pakistan Red Crescent Society Health and WASH Strategy 2026–2030.

The prototype demonstrates how evidence could be translated into geographic priorities, strategic choices, institutional alignment and implementation decisions.

## Prototype Modules

### 1. Where to Invest

An interactive geographic priority explorer designed to show how health, WASH, climate, displacement, branch readiness and PRCS reach may be considered together.

### 2. What to Prioritise

A strategic priority simulator that assesses interventions against:

- Humanitarian impact
- Equity
- Feasibility
- Affordability
- Financing potential
- Scalability
- Partnership potential
- Measurability

The simulator generates an indicative strategic treatment such as:

- Scale
- Strengthen
- Integrate
- Partner
- Redesign
- Pilot
- Deprioritise

### 3. Is PRCS Ready?

An organisational alignment diagnostic using the McKinsey 7S Framework as a supporting analytical lens.

The seven elements are:

- Strategy
- Structure
- Systems
- Shared Values
- Style
- Staff
- Skills

Shared Values are positioned at the centre because they influence all other organisational elements.

## Purpose

The purpose of this prototype is to demonstrate how the proposed consultancy may translate institutional evidence into practical leadership decision tools.

It is not presented as a completed PRCS assessment or official strategy platform.

## Important Disclaimer

This proof of concept uses illustrative placeholder data.

It does not represent:

- Assessed PRCS institutional performance
- Official provincial vulnerability rankings
- Approved strategic priorities
- Validated branch readiness scores
- Confirmed investment decisions

Final indicators, datasets, weights, geographic layers, institutional scores and user-access arrangements would be agreed with PRCS during inception and populated using verified evidence.
